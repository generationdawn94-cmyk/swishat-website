— Artist Bio

Swisha's back this summer.

The Ottawa-born hip-hop artist first made his name bringing realness and lyricism back to the rap game: a raw but highly polished style built on smart, conscious flow and modern production. He toured and collaborated with hip-hop icon Ghostface Killah, supported Machine Gun Kelly on the road, and shared stages with names like French Montana, Big Sean, and Wu-Tang Clan — building a global audience and a following that runs especially deep in Germany, where his music found a second home.

Then he stepped back.

The years that followed were not quiet ones. They were years of loss, reflection, and reckoning — the kind that either ends an artist or remakes him. Swisha T came out the other side remade. The new music is the sound of that transformation: still hard-hitting, still unflinching, but anchored now by something steadier. Where the early catalog chased proving grounds, this chapter is about peace, accountability, and faith — themes written plainly across the titles themselves: "Pray," "Losin Time," "Did You Worse."

The lead single, "Pray," sets the tone for the return — a statement of intent from an artist who has stopped running from his own story and started telling it on his own terms. It arrives alongside a deep well of new material spanning the introspective and the melodic, the confessional and the defiant.

With listeners still tuned in and a loyal core that never left, Swisha T's reemergence isn't a comeback in the usual sense. It's a continuation — picking the pen back up with a steadier hand and more to say than ever. For longtime fans and new listeners alike, this is the most honest version of the artist yet.
